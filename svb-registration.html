What is the AEO Programme?

The Authorized Economic Operator (AEO) programme is an initiative by Indian Customs to recognize and reward businesses that maintain a highly compliant and secure international supply chain. Obtaining AEO status establishes your company as a "trusted partner" in the eyes of Customs.

Key Benefits of AEO Status

  • ✔ Direct Port Delivery (DPD): Bypass CFS delays and receive containers directly from the port.
  • ✔ Deferred Duty Payment: Pay customs duties bi-monthly rather than per shipment, vastly improving cash flow.
  • ✔ Faster Assessment: Priority processing of Bills of Entry and reduced physical examinations.
  • ✔ Lower Bank Guarantees: Drastically reduced or zero bank guarantee requirements for customs bonds.
  • ✔ Faster Drawbacks: Expedited processing of duty drawbacks and IGST refunds.
  • ✔ International Recognition: Benefit from Mutual Recognition Agreements (MRAs) with other countries.

Documents Required for AEO Registration

To initiate the AEO application process, the following core documents must be prepared and verified. Depending on whether you are applying for AEO T1, T2, or T3, additional specific annexures may be required.

  • Certificate of Incorporation or Registration
  • Import Export Code (IEC) Certificate
  • GST Registration Certificate
  • PAN Card of the Company
  • List of Directors/Partners with their IDs
  • Audited Balance Sheets for the last 3 financial years
  • Income Tax Returns (ITR) for the last 3 financial years
  • Site Plan of the premises (Customs bonded areas, factories, etc.)
  • Details of security protocols and CCTV surveillance
  • Proof of legal compliance (No pending show-cause notices)
  • Logistics process flow charts and Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs)
