What is EPR?

Extended Producer Responsibility (EPR) is a critical environmental policy introduced by the Central Pollution Control Board (CPCB). It mandates that Producers, Importers, and Brand Owners (PIBOs) take financial and physical responsibility for the end-of-life disposal or recycling of their products.

Customs authorities now strictly enforce EPR registration at ports. Consignments lacking valid EPR certificates for Plastic Packaging, E-Waste, or Batteries face immediate detention.

Categories Requiring EPR

Plastic Packaging

Applies to importers of plastic packaging material or products wrapped in plastic. Includes rigid, flexible, multi-layered, and compostable plastics.

E-Waste

Mandatory for IT equipment, consumer electronics, consumer electricals, and their components/spares being imported into India.

Battery Waste

Required for importers of all types of batteries, regardless of chemistry, shape, or weight (including equipment with built-in batteries).

Documents Required for Registration

To secure your EPR certificate from the CPCB portal, the following documentation is mandatory:

  • Import Export Code (IEC)
  • GST Registration Certificate
  • PAN Card of Company & Authorized Signatory
  • Aadhaar Card of Authorized Signatory
  • Company Incorporation Certificate / Partnership Deed
  • Detailed Excel breakdown of imported quantities (historic and projected)
  • Digital Signature Certificate (DSC)
  • Tie-up agreements with registered recyclers (PWPs) - We can facilitate this
